About

A price memory that works for you, not the store.

PricePrint is a private grocery price tracker built by Artivicolab. You log what you pay; it builds a personal price history, flags fake sales, and shows your real inflation — and the prices you log stay on your device.

Why we built it

Grocery prices changed faster than anyone could keep track of, and the tools meant to explain it weren't built for you. The "inflation rate" on the news is a national average of a basket you don't buy. The "SALE" tag on the shelf is sometimes priced above what you paid last month. The only person with the data to catch any of this is you — but nobody was giving you a fast way to keep it.

So PricePrint does one thing well: it makes logging a price in the aisle faster than reading the tag, then turns those logs into answers. Is this sale real? Which store is actually cheapest on the things I buy? Is my grocery bill rising faster than the official number? All from prices you typed, not a panel you were sorted into.

How your data works

Everything you log — items, prices, stores, shopping lists — is written to a private database inside your own browser. There is no PricePrint server holding it; we couldn't read your price history if we wanted to. You can export it as JSON or CSV any time, and wipe it instantly. For the full detail on cookies and the analytics we use to count page visits, see our privacy page.

Who's behind it

PricePrint is made by Artivicolab, a small studio building privacy-first tools that earn their keep without harvesting the people who use them. We'd rather ship something genuinely useful and free than something that quietly monetizes your data.
