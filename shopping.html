Add items, see what they typically cost at the cheapest store you've shopped, get a running total before you leave the house. Check items off as you go — and log what you actually paid with one tap.

How it works

For every item on your list, PricePrint looks up the single lowest price you've ever logged for it, and which store you logged it at. That becomes the line estimate. The store recommendation is the store with that lowest price — not an average, not an advertised flyer price. Just the cheapest deal you actually witnessed.

The running total at the bottom shows what your basket should cost if you buy each item at its cheapest known store. If you split across multiple stores, the breakdown tells you how to split. Check an item as you put it in the cart, then tap "log it" to capture the real price you paid — which feeds back into the next list's estimates.

FAQ
What if an item isn't in my price history yet?
You can still add it as a text-only entry — it shows up on the list with "no price yet." Once you log a price for it, the estimate fills in for next time.
Does it remember the list between visits?
Yes — the active list is stored in IndexedDB on your device. Clear it manually when you're done with a trip, or leave items for next time.
What if I always buy a specific item at a specific store, even if it's not the cheapest?
The recommendation surfaces the cheapest known store, but nothing stops you from buying it elsewhere — the "log it" step records whatever price/store you actually paid. PricePrint learns from your real behavior, not from where it told you to shop.
Can I share my list with someone else?
No — there's no sync, no share link, no cloud. Your list lives on this device only. (Use export/import if you really need to move it.)
